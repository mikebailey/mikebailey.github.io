The Social Connectedness Index (SCI), built with [Johannes Stroebel](https://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~jstroebe/) and collaborators at NYU, measures the relative probability of friendship between people in different locations. The dataset has been used by researchers across economics, political science, public health, and sociology to study migration, trade, the spread of COVID-19, knowledge diffusion, and inequality.

**Related papers:**

- *The Economic Effects of Social Networks: Evidence from the Housing Market.* *Journal of Political Economy* 126(6), 2018. [[paper]](https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/doi/10.1086/700073)
- *House Price Beliefs and Mortgage Leverage Choice.* *Review of Economic Studies* 86(6), 2019. [[paper]](https://academic.oup.com/restud/article/86/6/2403/5306066)
- *Social Connectedness: Measurement, Determinants, and Effects.* *Journal of Economic Perspectives* 32(3), 2018. [[paper]](https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.32.3.259)
